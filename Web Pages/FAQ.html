FAQ (Rig Maker)

Q: How much do I need to spend on a PC?
A: What ever fits your budget, there is something for everyone.

Q: I don't know how to build a PC, what do I do?
A: Building a PC is a fun puzzle, it's simple and you can find guides all over the intrenet explaning in detail. If you really don't want to, you can take your parts to the nearest computer repair shop and they will help you.

Q: Is there warrenty on my PC? What do I do if a part fails?
A: Each individual part has it's own warrenty, you can contant the seller for that specific part and they usually have a warrenty of 1 year minimum.

Q: What part should I spend the most money on?
A: All parts have their own task. If you priorotize video editing and process heavy tasks, you should focus more on CPU. If your intrest is storing a lot of data and you don't use much else, focus on storage. If your focus is on games and 3D rendering, focus on GPU.

Q: My PC wont start after building it/
A: Trace the cables coming from your Power Supply and make sure they are all fully inserted. Make sure your Power Supply switch is ON.

Q: My PC keeps randomly shutting down with a blue screen right before.
A: At the bottom of the blue screen you can find a code that will help you narrow down the issue source. Re-install your RAM and make sure you hear both ends click.

Q: Do I need to buy a CPU cooler?
A: AMD provides CPU Coolers with their CPUs and they are quite sufficant. If you plan to Overclock your CPU or choose an Intel CPU, then yes, you should get a CPU Cooler.

Q: Is it ok to leave my PC running 24/7?
A: Yes, it will not harm your hardware at all. However, it will increase your electricity bill, but very slightly.
